# MSP Contract Red Flags

A quick checklist for reviewing your Managed Service Provider contract — the kind of thing that's easy to miss when IT isn't your day job.

- **Auto-renewal with a long notice period.** 90+ days' notice to cancel, buried in a clause nobody reads, is the single most common way businesses get stuck.
- **No named SLA response times** for priority levels — "we'll get to it" isn't a commitment.
- **Vague scope on "unlimited support."** Ask exactly what's excluded (project work, out-of-hours, third-party software issues) before you rely on it.
- **No data portability clause.** If you leave, can you actually get your data and configs out in a usable format, and how long does that take?
- **Bundled hardware/licensing markups with no visibility.** Ask for a breakdown — cost-plus vs. flat margin matters when you're trying to budget.
- **No named escalation path.** If your day-to-day contact isn't delivering, who do you go to, and is that written down anywhere?
- **Security responsibilities left ambiguous.** Patching, backups, and incident response should each have a named owner — "the MSP handles security" is not specific enough to hold anyone accountable.

If more than two or three of these are true of your current contract, it's worth a proper review before the next renewal date creeps up on you.
